A valuable bond among collaborators can often prove to be rewarding on both ends. In my personal and work life, I do what I can to provide a welcoming environment and hope that my counterpart does the same.

Even the most distant acquaintances deserve this basic right. In my most recent internship at Real Staffing, San Francisco, I learned the power of rapport. Seeing as how Real is a recruiting firm, communication is engraved in everyone’s job description.

On one of my first calls I received I was, admittedly, incredibly nervous. However, after we moved past the awkward greetings, I began to unearth more about the man’s personality, not just his resume builders.

I even discovered he was Iranian, just like me, which became an instant connection. Once I was able to open the gates of conversation and speak naturally (in conversational Farsi), I felt more compelled to help him in his cause and he was more adamant on working only with me and my team.

We were dedicated to one another even though he was one client out of many. I learned that everyone involved in a business is an integral part of how the company operates, and for that they deserve to be treated as such.
