Had I not questioned the norm a few years back, I may not have had the opportunity to be sitting here today.

Towards the end of my senior year of high school I was diagnosed with papillary cancer of the thyroid. This gland plays a huge role in regulating the human body as it controls the body’s energy, hormones, and metabolism.

So I did what everyone else would have done—step one: remove thyroid, step two: bedridden on painkillers, step three: radiation therapy. But nothing worked. I needed another surgery.

I endured so many natural remedies you could have packaged me up and sold me at Whole Foods. For six weeks my body was flushed with a rigorous herbal cleanse.

The torture was worth it though when the new doctor told me I was finally cancer-free. And with that, I now know that there is always another way to solve what may seem like an unsolvable issue. You just need the courage and conviction to take the path less traveled.
